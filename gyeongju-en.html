Introduction

    Overview

    Gyeongju, once the ancient capital of Silla, is full of cultural sites throughout the city. Silla developed from the Gyeongsangdo region in the 1st century BC, and successively eliminated Goguryeo and Baekje, and became the first unified country on the Korean Peninsula in the 7th century. Gyeongju, the ancient capital for a thousand years, has the most Korean natural landscape and sites from the Silla era. It attracts 8-9 million domestic and foreign tourists every year. The most famous attractions are Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram, Gyeongju National Museum, Dalingwon (Tenma Tomb), Fenhuangsa Temple, Cheomseongdae, Gyrin, Huanglong Temple Site, Linhai Temple Site (Goose Duck Pond), Baoshi Pavilion Site, Nanshan Mountain, Bomen Tourist Group, etc. In 2000, the Gyeongju site was designated as a world cultural heritage by the International Conference on Monuments and Sites (a non-governmental organization established by UNESCO to protect international cultural relics).

    Culture

    Gyeongju was once the capital of Silla and has a history of thousands of years. The various legends of ancient Silla that have been passed down for thousands of years have cast a beautiful and mysterious veil on Gyeongju City.

    Environment

    Gyeongju City is surrounded by mountains on all sides, and is surrounded by confluence of rivers. To the east is the Sea of Japan (South Korea is called the East Sea).

Tourist time

    Tourist season

    Gyeongju has four distinct seasons and has obvious continental climate characteristics. The winter is cold and the summer is hot, so it is hotter than the coastal areas. The annual average temperature is 12.5 degrees, the rainfall is 1,157mm, and there are typhoons in summer.

    Best time to travel

    Gyeongju is a temperate climate zone with four distinct seasons. There are more sunny days in spring and autumn, and the climate is mild and comfortable. The summer is hot and the temperature is high, and the rainy season is from June to August. The winter is dry and sometimes the snowfall is cold. Therefore, the spring and autumn seasons are more suitable for play.
